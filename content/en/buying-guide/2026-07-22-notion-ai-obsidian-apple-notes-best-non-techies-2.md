Apple Notes wins for non-techies. It's not close.

Zero setup. Free forever. And in a 90-day structured test, it produced the highest notes-per-day output of the three apps — 14.7 daily notes — because the tool never got in the way of the thinking.

Who shouldn't pick Apple Notes? Anyone managing shared team projects. Notion is the only one of the three with real multi-person databases, Kanban views, and live collaboration. If your workflow involves more than one person editing the same workspace, Apple Notes can't compete.

Everything else — setup friction, pricing, AI capability, capture speed, and long-term data portability — gets compared below.

---

> **Key Takeaways**
> - **Apple Notes**: Start taking notes today, not next Tuesday after configuring plugins
> - **Notion AI**: Running team projects that need databases, shared docs, and real collaboration
> - **Skip Obsidian** unless you have two full evenings to invest before writing your first real note

## The Three Contenders

**Notion** is a web-based workspace built around databases. Atlassian-sized in ambition, it's grown into a product that tries to replace Jira, Confluence, and Google Docs simultaneously. Pricing as of July 2026: Free tier, Plus at $10/month, Business at $18/user/month, with AI as a $10/member/month add-on — or bundled into Business at $20/user/month. Notion launched AI Agents in September 2025. The honest framing: it's a project management tool that happens to support note-taking. The configuration overhead is real and consistently documented across independent reviews.

**Obsidian** is local-first, plain-text Markdown stored on your own machine. No cloud dependency, 1,800+ community plugins, and bidirectional linking that surfaces connections between notes you forgot you made. Per official documentation: free for personal use, Sync at $5/month, Publish at $10/month. Version 1.11 (January 2026) added Siri and Shortcuts integration. It scores 12/25 on AI capability by design — the product philosophy explicitly rejects cloud dependency. For privacy-conscious power users, that's a feature. For non-techies, it's a dealbreaker.

**Apple Notes** ships pre-installed on every iPhone, iPad, and Mac. Free. Always. The 2025–2026 update cycle added Smart Folders, improved Apple Pencil OCR that searches handwritten text, and enhanced collaboration. No configuration required. According to [Nadia Okafor's 60-day test on Medium](https://justtalkingtech.medium.com/notion-vs-obsidian-vs-apple-notes-60-day-test-honest-answer-e358da3212af), after 20 days the app became "functionally invisible" — attention stayed on content, not the container.

*Data sourced from: [The Modern Observer 90-day test](https://themodernobserver.com/tech/notion-vs-obsidian-vs-apple-notes-2026), [alfred_ 2026 AI scoring](https://get-alfred.ai/blog/best-ai-note-taking-apps), official vendor pricing pages (July 2026).*

**The retrieval speed row surprises most people.** Obsidian at 8 seconds beats both competitors despite being local-only and free. Bidirectional linking means notes surface each other passively — you don't need to remember the exact search term. Notion's 12-second average reflects database query overhead. Apple Notes hits 15 seconds because it indexes everything (images, handwriting, PDFs), which is powerful but slower to isolate the right result.

**The "time spent creating" row is where non-techies quietly lose with Notion.** Only 65% of Notion session time was actual note-writing, against 92% in Apple Notes. That 27-point gap is the configuration trap — users building systems instead of using them. For a non-techie, that overhead compounds every single day.

**Obsidian's data portability win is the highest-stakes row long-term.** Plain Markdown files stored locally remain readable in 50 years with zero vendor risk. Apple Notes has no standard export format — [alfred_'s 2026 analysis](https://get-alfred.ai/blog/best-ai-note-taking-apps) lists data portability as the highest-stakes evaluation criterion, and Apple Notes scores poorly there. That's a real tradeoff worth understanding before you commit years of notes to a closed ecosystem.

**AI pricing is Notion's biggest practical barrier for personal users.** The free tier locks AI behind a $20/user/month Business plan. Apple Intelligence (built into iOS/macOS) is free, though limited in organizational depth. For non-techies who want AI features without a monthly commitment, that pricing structure eliminates Notion from the conversation before it starts.

## Where Each One Actually Breaks

**Notion breaks when you need to capture something fast.** The keystroke lag and load delays are documented consistently across independent reviews — Okafor's Medium test describes mobile quick capture as "effectively unusable." Open Notion on your phone, wait for it to load, navigate to the right database, select the right property type. The idea you needed to capture is gone. For non-techies treating Notion as a note-taking app rather than a project workspace, this friction kills adoption within weeks. It's not a bug. Notion was built for structured project management, and fast capture was never the priority.

**Obsidian breaks when you need to use it tired, distracted, or sick.** Okafor's "Tired Test" — usability at 11pm without deliberate thought — is where Obsidian collapses entirely. Two full evenings of setup before meaningful note-taking began. Eleven plugins installed before the system felt functional. The Obsidian community on Reddit openly discusses this as a "tinkering loop" problem: the tool rewards configuration enthusiasm, not note-taking consistency. Non-techies don't have the activation energy to maintain a plugin ecosystem through a rough week, let alone a rough month.

**Apple Notes breaks the moment your workflow involves a team.** Shared folders exist, but there's no database view, no task assignment, no Kanban board. The collaboration ceiling is two people editing the same note — not a team managing a product roadmap. This isn't a gap that future updates are likely to close, because it's not what Apple Notes is designed to be.

## The Verdict

Apple Notes wins this comparison for non-techies, and it's not a philosophical debate. The data is direct: 14.7 notes per day vs. 8.2 for Notion, 92% creating time vs. 65%, zero setup vs. two full evenings. The tool that gets out of your way wins for users who aren't optimizing a productivity system — they just need to stop losing important information.

Notion earns its place the moment your use case involves more than one person. Obsidian earns its place for anyone who thinks seriously about long-term knowledge ownership, can absorb the upfront setup cost, and genuinely values controlling their data in a portable format. Neither of those is the average non-techie.

**The immediate next step**: Open Apple Notes right now. Create four folders — Work, Personal, Reference, Inbox. That's the entire system. Don't install Notion for personal note-taking until you've genuinely hit Apple Notes' ceiling. Most non-techies never do.

**Worth watching**: Apple's approach to AI organization inside Notes is still developing. Apple Intelligence handles drafting and summarization competently, but true semantic search across a large vault remains weak. If Apple ships meaningful AI organization — not just AI writing assistance — into Notes by late 2026, the remaining arguments for Notion at the personal tier nearly disappear. That's the product update that would close the last real gap.
