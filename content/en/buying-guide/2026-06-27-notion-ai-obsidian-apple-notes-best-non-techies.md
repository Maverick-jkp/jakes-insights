Apple Notes wins for non-techies. No setup, no subscriptions, no configuration rabbit holes — it just works. The data backs this up: [a 30-day structured test by The Modern Observer](https://themodernobserver.com/tech/notion-vs-obsidian-vs-apple-notes-2026) tracked 14.7 notes created per day in Apple Notes versus only 8.2 in Notion. That gap isn't about features. It's about friction.

But Apple Notes isn't the right pick for everyone in this comparison. If you manage projects with multiple collaborators, track clients in a relational database, or need Kanban views and timeline filters — Notion AI is the only tool here that actually delivers those workflows. And if you're a researcher or writer building a long-term knowledge base, Obsidian's bidirectional linking has no real equivalent in the other two.

Dimensions covered below:

- **Setup time and learning curve**
- **Daily capture speed and friction**
- **AI features and practical value**
- **Data portability and lock-in risk**

> **TL;DR**
> - Use **Apple Notes** if: you want zero friction, free sync, and you're inside the Apple ecosystem
> - Use **Notion AI** if: you're managing projects, collaborating with a team, or need a relational database
> - Use **Obsidian** if: you're building a long-term knowledge graph and are comfortable with Markdown and plugin setup
> - Skip Obsidian if: you want AI assistance baked in or you're not willing to spend an afternoon on initial configuration

## The Contenders

**Apple Notes** ships free with every Apple device. No account required. The 2025–2026 updates added Smart Folders, improved handwritten OCR, and tag support — but it's still a fundamentally simple app. That's the point. [According to Nadia Okafor's 60-day test on Medium](https://justtalkingtech.medium.com/notion-vs-obsidian-vs-apple-notes-60-day-test-honest-answer-e358da3212af), the app became "mentally invisible during use." No competing app in this field earns that description.

**Notion AI** is Notion's product with its AI layer fully enabled. The Business tier at $20/user/month (restructured May 2025, per [alfred_'s 2026 market analysis](https://get-alfred.ai/blog/best-ai-note-taking-apps)) unlocks AI Agents — launched September 2025 — that can query Google Drive and Slack alongside your notes. The free tier works but caps file uploads at 5MB and excludes AI features. Notion's core strength is its database engine: Kanban, timeline, filtered views, linked properties. Nothing in this comparison touches it for structured project data.

**Obsidian** is a local-first Markdown editor with 1,800+ community plugins. Personal use is free. Encrypted sync costs $5/month. Version 1.11 (January 2026) added Siri and Shortcuts integration. Every note is a plain `.md` file stored on your device — which means zero lock-in and trivial migration. The trade-off: you configure everything yourself. Plugin selection, vault architecture, themes. [Okafor's test found Obsidian effective for roughly 10% of note-taking tasks](https://justtalkingtech.medium.com/notion-vs-obsidian-vs-apple-notes-60-day-test-honest-answer-e358da3212af) — specifically the deep, connective thinking work — and frustrating for the other 90%.

The most surprising row is time allocation. Notion users spent 35% of their total in-app time *organizing* rather than capturing or writing. That's not a workflow failure — it's the product doing exactly what it's designed for. But for someone who wants notes, not systems architecture, that ratio is an anchor.

Obsidian's retrieval speed of 8 seconds beats both competitors, but that number carries a real caveat: it assumes a user who has already invested time building a well-linked vault. Day one in Obsidian is slower, not faster.

The AI parity score deserves context. Apple's AI features are narrower — handwriting OCR, basic on-device search — but deeply integrated. Notion's AI requires the $20/month Business tier to unlock meaningfully. At the free tier, Notion's AI advantage essentially disappears. Both tools scoring 19/25 tells you something important: throwing money at AI features doesn't automatically mean better results.

Data portability is the row most non-techies ignore until they need it. Notion exports break database structures. Apple Notes has no native bulk export. Obsidian's plain Markdown means you can move everything to another app in minutes. That matters more than people realize — especially if the product you're locked into raises prices or shuts down.

## Where Each One Actually Breaks

**Apple Notes breaks when** you try to track anything relational. No database views, no linked properties, no filters across notes. If you're managing five active client projects with statuses, owners, and due dates — Apple Notes becomes a pile of text files. Users typically hit this wall after 3–6 months of growth and face migrating everything mid-workflow. It's painful.

**Notion AI breaks when** you need to capture something quickly in a low-connectivity environment or on mobile. The input lag [documented throughout Okafor's 20-day Notion test](https://justtalkingtech.medium.com/notion-vs-obsidian-vs-apple-notes-60-day-test-honest-answer-e358da3212af) is a real friction point — keyboard delay persisted across the entire evaluation period. Notion is also structurally cloud-dependent; offline mode exists but is unreliable for complex databases. The tool built for power users fails precisely when power users are away from reliable Wi-Fi.

**Obsidian breaks when** a non-technical user hits the initial configuration wall. Vault architecture, plugin selection, Markdown syntax during fast meetings — these aren't insurmountable problems, but they're real. The [alfred_ 2026 analysis scored Obsidian 12/25](https://get-alfred.ai/blog/best-ai-note-taking-apps) specifically because its design philosophy opts out of AI and sync features that most users now expect by default. A non-techie who installs Obsidian without a setup guide will likely abandon it within two weeks. The learning curve isn't steep — it's just invisible until you slam into it.

## The Verdict

**Apple Notes wins for non-techies.** The data is consistent across all three sources: highest daily note volume, lowest organizational overhead, zero cost, zero setup. For someone who wants to capture thoughts, track meetings, and find things quickly, no other app in this comparison comes close.

The exception holds. If your work involves managing projects with a team, tracking clients with structured data, or running async collaboration — Notion AI earns its $20/month. And if you're a researcher willing to invest a weekend in setup, Obsidian's knowledge graph pays compounding returns that neither competitor can match.

**The practical next step:** Open Apple Notes right now. Create four folders: Work, Personal, Projects, Archive. That's the setup. You're done. [The Modern Observer's test](https://themodernobserver.com/tech/notion-vs-obsidian-vs-apple-notes-2026) found this exact minimal structure outperformed heavily configured Notion and Obsidian vaults on daily capture volume within the first week.

One question worth watching: Notion AI Agents — currently querying Drive and Slack — are expanding their integration surface. If Notion solves the mobile input lag problem by late 2026, the daily capture gap with Apple Notes narrows significantly. Does AI-assisted organization eventually outweigh zero-friction capture? That's the real race. And right now, nobody has won it yet.
